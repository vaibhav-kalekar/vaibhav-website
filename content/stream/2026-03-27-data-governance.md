Data governance can be tedious, overbearing and complicated — especially for small teams who just want quick, reliable answers from their data.

LLMs are powerful but hallucinate. For governance purposes, that’s a dealbreaker.
So what if you grounded it strictly in your own metadata — no guessing, no fabrication, just your data answering for itself?

A weekend project to implement something like it? Open source, lightweight, and something most data engineers could spin up without a vendor conversation.

The future of data governance might not be bought. It might be built — cheaply, openly, and by engineers tired of waiting for the budget to be approved.

Would you trust an open source stack to govern your data platform?
