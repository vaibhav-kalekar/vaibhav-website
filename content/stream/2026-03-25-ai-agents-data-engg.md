AI Agents are quietly reshaping what it means to be a Data Engineer.

The work is shifting — less time optimising SQL and orchestrating pipelines, more time designing scalable frameworks and governance structures for AI to operate within.

That sounds like an evolution. But here's the uncomfortable part: the architectural judgment and domain knowledge that used to take years to develop? It's increasingly a prompt away.

The Data Engineering world is going to feel this shift all at once — and most teams are still heads down in the day-to-day to see it coming.

The engineers who will stay relevant are the ones who stop thinking in pipelines and start thinking in data products — outcomes that the business can actually depend on in production.

The semantic layer isn't a technical afterthought anymore. It's where engineering meets business value.

AI Agents make this more urgent, not less — because ungoverned agents sitting on top of poorly designed data products will fail loudly.

How are you preparing your data platform for the agent era?
